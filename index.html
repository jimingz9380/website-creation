Intro

The Merbear is a new species of bear that is quite unique compared to the other types of bears. It became a new species shortly after the polar bears went extinct. Studies have shown that as the Arctic melted, polar bears slowly started disappearing till it under-go Natural Selection from a random mutation which allows a bear to be born with fins better for surviving underwater. As time went on more of the polar bears took the features of a fish allowing it to have a slimmer body, less fur, back legs turned into fins, and gills. This allowed the bears to fully live underwater and use their front paws which still exist as good tools for hunting. The bear could only reach the Twilight Zone of the ocean and can’t go any further due to the lack of light.

Etymology (Origin)

The Merbear have been living in what was the Arctic now became part of the Arctic Ocean for about 100 years. Just like their predecessor, Ursus maritimus(polar bears) who had also adapted to the cold temperature, the Merbear had adapted to the abundance of water but lack of land. The reason for this lack of land would be when there was extreme heating that had happened 250 years ago over at the arctic which caused the majority of the glaciers to melt down and made the polar bears have a competition between each other over who can get to their prey first. After news was spread about this competition, scientists all over the world had made attempts to preserve this species by mutating them with some aspects of sea animals. This lead to a gradual change in these polar bears which from natural selection change them to what you see as a Merbear.
